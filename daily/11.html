Feeling lonely.

…

If I’m going to limit myself to just 4 hours of work per day (for now), I need to address the elephant in the room:

Free time and loneliness.

I don’t know what to do in my free time.

Having nothing to do and having no one to talk to is a deadly combination. My income doesn’t support joining something like Muay Thai.

I’m not willing to spend the extra money because it means extra time at my job.

List of things that would interest me.

  • Reading books.
  • Writing.
  • Journaling.
  • Reading blogs.
  • Reading indie hackers.
  • Running.
  • Calisthenics.
  • Walking.
  • Watching movies.

Literally anything would be better than the depressing feeling of knowing that I’ve only been doing task that require 10% of my brain power like scrolling the internet.

How would I even tackle loneliness?

Well what type of people do I even want to surround myself with?

I want to surround myself with people who interested in indie hacking or who are really into physical fitness. I want to find cracked calisthenics people and develop skills with them.

I know that in order to attract these people I have to take those aspects of my life more seriously.

Also I have to stop acting like a little pussy and be the one to initiate friendships. If I’m claiming that this is such a pervasive problem in my life then I need to take my loneliness seriously.

I need to get out there. I see people jogging all the time. I walk by people and see them doing calisthenics. Why can’t I say something to them? I talk to people all day at my job so why can’t I shoot my shot and see if I can make a friend with similar values?

This feeling sucks. I miss hanging out with my best friend.

Rest in piece.
